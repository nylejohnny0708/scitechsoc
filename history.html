The History of Climate Change

Understanding the origins and evolution of climate change is crucial for addressing this global challenge.

Key Milestones

1824 - Discovery of Greenhouse Effect

Joseph Fourier first describes how atmospheric gases trap heat, laying the foundation for climate science.

1896 - First Climate Prediction

Svante Arrhenius calculates that doubling CO₂ could raise temperatures 5-6°C, remarkably close to modern estimates.

1958 - Keeling Curve Begins

Charles David Keeling starts continuous CO₂ measurements at Mauna Loa, revealing the steady rise in atmospheric carbon dioxide.

1988 - IPCC Established

The Intergovernmental Panel on Climate Change is formed to assess climate change science and impacts.

2015 - Paris Agreement

195 countries commit to limit global warming to well below 2°C above pre-industrial levels.

Root Causes of Climate Change

Human Activities

  • Burning Fossil Fuels: Coal, oil and gas produce greenhouse gases when burned for energy
  • Deforestation: Trees absorb CO₂, so cutting them down worsens climate change
  • Transportation: Cars, trucks, ships and planes burn fossil fuels
  • Buildings: Energy used for heating, cooling and powering buildings

Natural Factors

  • Solar Variations: Changes in the sun's energy output can affect climate
  • Volcanic Activity: Eruptions can temporarily cool the climate by releasing aerosols
  • Ocean Currents: Changes in circulation patterns can affect regional climates
  • Water Vapor: The most abundant greenhouse gas that amplifies warming

Global Impacts

Environmental

  • • Rising sea levels
  • • More extreme weather
  • • Ecosystem disruption
  • • Species extinction

Economic

  • • $54 trillion in damages by 2100
  • • Reduced agricultural yields
  • • Infrastructure damage
  • • Increased insurance costs

Humanitarian

  • • Increased health risks
  • • Food and water scarcity
  • • Climate migration
  • • Conflict over resources
